Our Rich History

1985
Foundation & Early Years

Monsignor Bala Secondary School was founded in 1985 by the Catholic Diocese of Arua, under the visionary leadership of Monsignor Bala Dracua. The school began with just 120 students and 8 teachers in temporary structures, driven by a mission to provide quality education to the youth of Adjumani and surrounding areas.

1990
First Permanent Buildings

The construction of the first permanent classroom blocks was completed, marking a significant milestone in the school's development. The student population grew to 300, and the school gained recognition for its academic excellence and discipline.

2000
Expansion & Modernization

The new millennium brought significant expansion with the addition of science laboratories, a library, and administrative offices. The school introduced computer studies and began participating in national academic competitions, consistently achieving top rankings.

2010
Digital Age Integration

MBSS embraced the digital age with the establishment of a modern computer laboratory and internet connectivity. The school launched its first website and began integrating technology into the curriculum, preparing students for the 21st century.

2020
Resilience & Innovation

Despite global challenges, MBSS demonstrated remarkable resilience by quickly adapting to remote learning technologies and implementing innovative teaching methods. The school maintained its commitment to quality education while ensuring student safety and well-being.

2025
Excellence Continues

Today, MBSS stands as a beacon of educational excellence with over 800 students, 45 qualified teachers, and state-of-the-art facilities. The school continues to produce outstanding graduates who excel in universities and contribute meaningfully to society.
