Giving Back to the Community

MBSS alumni have embraced the spirit of service, dedicating their time, skills, and resources to uplift their communities. Their commitment to social responsibility reflects the values instilled during their time at our school and continues to make a positive impact across Uganda and beyond.

Making a Difference

Mary Adongo

Founder, Adjumani Girls Education Foundation

Class of 2006 - Mary established a foundation that provides scholarships and mentorship to underprivileged girls in Adjumani District. Her organization has supported over 200 girls to complete their secondary education and has built two dormitories for female students.

Peter Ojok

Director, West Nile Health Initiative

Class of 2007 - Peter leads a health initiative that provides free medical camps, health education, and clean water projects across West Nile region. His organization has reached over 50,000 people with essential health services and built 15 boreholes in rural communities.

"MBSS taught me that with privilege comes responsibility. Using my medical training to serve those who cannot afford healthcare is my way of giving back to society."

Samuel Draku

Coordinator, Youth Skills Development Program

Class of 2009 - Samuel runs a vocational training program that teaches practical skills to out-of-school youth. His program offers courses in carpentry, tailoring, mechanics, and computer literacy, helping young people become self-reliant and productive members of society.
